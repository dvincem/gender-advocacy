What is Gender and Development?

Gender and Development (GAD) is a holistic approach to development that focuses on addressing gender inequalities by recognizing how gender roles, power relations, and social norms affect individuals differently.

GAD emerged as a critical framework in development theory and practice, shifting focus from simply ‚Äúadding women‚Äù to transforming the structures that perpetuate inequality.

Key Elements of GAD

Gender Relations: Power and resource distribution between genders.

Intersectionality: Gender intersects with class, race, age, and ability.

Structural Change: Focus on systems, not just individuals.

Participation: Inclusive decision-making at all levels.

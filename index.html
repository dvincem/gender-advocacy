What is Gender and Development (GAD)?

Gender and Development (GAD) is a framework that recognizes the social, economic, and political differences experienced by people of different genders. It promotes equal rights, opportunities, and participation in development.

Key Gender Issues

Gender Discrimination

Unequal treatment based on gender that affects access to education, employment, and leadership opportunities.

Gender-Based Violence

Acts of violence rooted in power imbalance, often affecting women and marginalized genders.

Economic Inequality

Wage gaps and limited access to resources continue to affect gender equality.

Why Gender and Development Matters

  • Promotes social justice and human rights
  • Ensures equal participation in nation-building
  • Supports sustainable and inclusive development
  • Reduces poverty and inequality

Our Advocacy Goals

This advocacy aims to raise awareness, educate communities, and support policies that protect gender equality.

Awareness

Educate individuals about gender issues and their impact.

Protection

Support laws and policies that defend gender rights.

Empowerment

Encourage equal participation in leadership and decision-making.

What You Can Do

Everyone plays a role in promoting gender equality. Small actions can create meaningful change.

  1. Respect all genders
  2. Speak up against discrimination
  3. Support gender-inclusive policies
  4. Educate others
